# Quick Thoughts on Knowledge Graphs

The idea of using **wikilinks** (`[[like this]]`) to build a personal knowledge graph
is powerful. Each link creates a bidirectional relationship.

## Why It Matters

- Every note becomes a **node** in a graph
- Links are **edges** — they carry meaning
- Backlinks let you discover *unexpected connections*

## Tools That Do This Well

1. **Obsidian** — the gold standard for local-first wikilink graphs
2. **Logseq** — outliner-first with strong graph support
3. **Roam** — pioneered bidirectional linking

> The graph isn't just a visualization — it's a *thinking tool*.
> Connections emerge that you wouldn't find in a flat folder structure.

See [[Graph Theory Basics]] for the underlying math.
